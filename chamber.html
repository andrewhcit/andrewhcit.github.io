Felix Mendelssohn (1809-1847, Germany) – String Octet
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9JTH4ZdJcjk

  • Mendelssohn's Octet, composed when he was 16, was his first major success and its remarkable maturity is the reason he is considered by some to have been a greater prodigy than Mozart.
  • Mendelssohn was the conductor who brought Bach out of obscurity. This was still years in the future when he composed his Octet, but his lifelong interest in the Baroque era is evident in the fourth movement, which directly quotes a melody from the Hallelujah Chorus in Handel's Messiah.
